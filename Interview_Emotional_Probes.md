# NF2 Interview — Bounded Emotional Probes

**Principle:** Emotion is a *design quality*, not a feature, in this MVP. So every emotional question must map to a buildable design output (tone, color, pacing, layout, microcopy). If a probe can't change one of those, it's noise — cut it.

**Don't ask:** open-ended "tell me about your emotional journey with NF2." It produces feeling without a decision attached.

**Do ask** (each tied to an output):

| # | Probe | Design output it feeds |
|---|-------|------------------------|
| 1 | "When a new scan result comes in, walk me through what you feel in the first minute." | Disclosure pacing — whether the tool reveals the number immediately or lets the user choose; opening-screen tone. |
| 2 | "If you saw a chart of a tumour growing over time, how would you want that shown?" | Data-viz color and framing — avoiding alarm-red defaults; neutral annotation; what to emphasise vs soften. |
| 3 | "What's the feeling walking into a *new* specialist's office with your history?" | Doctor-summary layout — confidence cues that reduce the patient's sense of vulnerability and being unprepared. |
| 4 | "What makes you stop tracking, or avoid opening your records for a while?" | Friction reduction, empty/return states, non-judgmental copy (no "you haven't logged in 30 days"). |
| 5 | "Which words feel clinical-but-safe to you, and which feel cold or frightening?" *(show 4–5 examples)* | Microcopy lexicon — terminology and labels across the interface. |
| 6 | "When does a medical app start to feel like too much?" | Layout density and progressive disclosure — how much to show at once. |

**Read-out:** synthesise answers into a short "affective design brief" — a palette direction, a tone-of-voice note, and 2–3 disclosure/pacing rules — that sits alongside the functional persona work. That keeps the emotional layer actionable and visibly separate from the feature set.
