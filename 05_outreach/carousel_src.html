Finding 02 · The consultation bottleneck
71%

hit communication breakdowns when they face a new doctor.

The #1 cause isn't medical — it's informational: records too scattered to show years of progression inside a 10-minute consult (37%).

And 76% live with partial or total hearing loss — so the answer must be visual by default, not spoken.

Finding 03 · What people say ≠ what they do
They say
50%

picked a plain-language research library as a top-2 must-have feature.

They do
8%

actually consult literature when a new symptom appears. Most ask their doctor or their patient group instead.

Stated preference favors features people imagine using. Behavior points at the rare, high-stakes moment: handing a new doctor your whole history, fast. That's the moment worth designing for.

Honest limits & what's next

n=38 is directional, not statistical. But NF2 affects ~1 in 33,000 people — 38 respondents and 29 interview volunteers from one of the hardest-to-reach patient communities is access most research teams never get.

Next: 5–8 in-depth interviews, then a concept prototype — a patient-owned disease timeline that generates a doctor-ready one-page summary.
